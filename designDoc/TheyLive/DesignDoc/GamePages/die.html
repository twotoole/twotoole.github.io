Die Hard Trilogy

Notes
  • Die Hard 1
  • Score + level based
  • Run around level to kill enemies and release hostages, number of enemies is in the top right and when a hostage escapes their portrait is added to the pile in top right.
    • this is all related to score.
  • seems to shoot at enemy as long as your pointed generally at them.
  • UI contains:
    • Health (indicated by cop badge) and lives remaining.
    • grenade count
    • Score
    • Ammo (default pistol has 15 capacity but will always reload)
    • mini map in bottom left
      • enemy is red, hostage is blue.
      • shows walls, and door are indicated by a space in the wall.
    • indicated when an enemy is aiming at you and their direction in bottom left.
  • Level Boss has big red BOSS over them.
  • level 3:
  • when you reach end of level you trigger a bomb and a timer starts for you to escape
    • direction to the elevator (the exit of the level), is signposted throughout the level.
  • picks up new rifle, when ammo runs out, the gun disappears and your back to default pistol.
    • doesnt seem like switching weapons is a thing.

What was good/interesting?

    They did a good job with the aesthetic and design to evoke the film. I think even if you didn't know this was a game based on the film Die Hard, a player would be able to realise that on their own.
    • Character model was good (tank top, no shoes)
    • starting and ending mission with elevator and alkways referring to the floor the player is on
    However the gameplay and tone that creates is very different that the film creating some dissonance. The gameplay is very fast and you're mowing through people which is obviously very different form the slow pace and stealth like elements of the film.
